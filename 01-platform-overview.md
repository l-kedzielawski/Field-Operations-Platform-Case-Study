# Platform Overview

## What this platform actually is

`Northfield Ops` is an internal business application for running field operations more reliably.

The company and product name used here are intentionally anonymized for the public case study.

It helps the business manage workers, supervisors, projects, compliance documents, field submissions, attendance, and admin oversight in one place.

That matters because before the platform, those activities were scattered across messages, calls, spreadsheets, inboxes, local folders, and memory.

## The problem it solves

Field-heavy businesses often have the same pattern: the real work happens on site, but the information about that work arrives late, in the wrong format, or through the wrong channel.

That leads to avoidable friction:

- supervisors spend time chasing updates,
- work evidence is inconsistent,
- document issues surface too late,
- sensitive records are harder to control safely,
- attendance records become hard to trust,
- and management never gets one clear picture.

This platform was built to solve that operating problem, not just to make admin screens look better.

## The four connected parts

### 1. Work supervision

Workers submit structured reports from the field. Those submissions are tied to projects, supervisors, evidence, and approval status.

That turns informal updates into something reviewable and traceable.

![Field submission form placeholder](screenshots/03-field-submission-form.png)

### 2. Document governance

Each worker has a document profile shaped by their company type and compliance path. The system tracks required files, approval state, expiry dates, missing items, and expiring records.

This is not just file storage. It is a live compliance workflow with controlled access to sensitive records.

![Document center placeholder](screenshots/06-document-center-overview.png)

### 3. Attendance

Workers clock in and out against real projects and supervisors. The system stores time entries, supports review, and keeps a trail when corrections are needed.

That makes attendance part of operations, not a separate after-the-fact spreadsheet process.

### 4. Operational atlas

Projects can be placed on a map and viewed alongside assignments and activity. This gives management a quick operational view that is much stronger than a list of jobs in a spreadsheet.

![Operational atlas placeholder](screenshots/01-operational-atlas.png)

## Why the pieces matter more together

The platform is valuable because the modules reinforce each other.

- Projects give submissions and attendance real context.
- Documents determine whether certain actions are allowed.
- Access controls determine who can review, retrieve, and change sensitive records.
- Approvals turn activity into governed records.
- The map and admin views turn isolated records into operational visibility.

That is the difference between a set of tools and an actual operating system for the business.

## A simple example

One worker can be tied to:

- assigned projects,
- assigned supervisors,
- required compliance documents,
- field evidence,
- and attendance records.

Because those things are connected, the platform can do more than store information. It can enforce rules.

For example, a worker can be blocked from submitting work if the required documents for that workflow are not valid. Office staff can see whether records are pending, approved, rejected, expiring, expired, or missing, and sensitive files are handled through more controlled access patterns than simple public links.
